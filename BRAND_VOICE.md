# BRAND_VOICE.md — Chaunda C. Dallas language library

Companion to CLAUDE.md. Pull from these when writing copy so nothing sounds repetitive.
Rotate — never reuse the same origin line, or the "28 years" framing, twice on one page.

## Guardrails (carry over from CLAUDE.md)
- The flagship tagline is sacred. Keep it powerful by NOT overusing it: "I didn't leave healthcare. I learned to defend it."
- Never frame her as transitioning / pivoting / crossing over / leaving. She never left.
- When a number is used, it's 28 years (or "nearly three decades" / "decades") — but don't lead with the number every time.

## Flagship + companion
- "I didn't leave healthcare. I learned to defend it." — flagship; use sparingly, keep it hitting hard.
- Companion, same meaning without repeating the flagship: "The mission never changed. I simply found another way to protect it."

## Mission-focus variations
- I still serve healthcare. I just protect it differently.
- My mission never changed. My tools did.
- Healthcare is still my calling. Cybersecurity is how I answer it today.
- I don't see healthcare and cybersecurity as separate careers — I see one continuous mission.
- The uniforms changed. The mission never did.
- I didn't change careers. I changed the scale of who I protect.

## Patient-focus variations
- Every cyber decision eventually becomes a patient-safety decision.
- Patient safety doesn't stop at the bedside.
- Behind every healthcare cyber incident is a human story.
- Cybersecurity is another form of patient care.
- Technology may be the target, but people are what matter.

## Digital First Responder language
- The emergency may be digital, but the people are real.
- Clinical triage taught me how to respond to cyber crises.
- Every incident deserves the same discipline as an emergency room.
- I bring an ER mindset to cyber defense.
- Digital First Responder isn't a title — it's a way of thinking.

## Healthcare-experience variations (avoid repeating "28 years in the ER")
- My perspective was forged in emergency departments long before I worked in cybersecurity.
- Before protecting healthcare systems, I spent decades caring for the people inside them.
- Long before I analyzed cyber risk, I was managing clinical risk.
- I learned crisis management at the bedside before applying it to cyber defense.
- My foundation isn't just cybersecurity — it's healthcare.
- Emergency medicine taught me that preparation saves lives.

## Signature lines (openers, bios, intros)
- I once responded to medical emergencies. Today I help healthcare prepare for digital ones.
- From bedside resilience to cyber resilience.
- I spent years caring for patients. Today I help protect the systems they depend on.
- Every career chapter has prepared me for this one.

## Usage note
This is a menu, not a script. Strongest: the flagship, its companion, and the DFR / patient-safety pairs. A few mission lines are more generic ("Different role. Same purpose.") — use those as light touches, never as a headline.
